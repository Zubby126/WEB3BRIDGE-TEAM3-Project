What is Project Management?

"Project management is the process of planning, executing, and overseeing a project to achieve specific goals within a set timeframe and budget. It involves applying various tools, techniques, and knowledge areas to ensure that projects are completed successfully, meeting their objectives while managing constraints such as time, cost, and quality.:"

Key Concepts of Project Management

  1. Project:

    • A project is a temporary endeavor undertaken to create a unique product, service, or result. It has a clear beginning and end, defined objectives, and specific deliverables.

  2. Project Management:

    • Project management is the application of knowledge, skills, tools, and techniques to project activities to meet the project requirements. It typically involves a series of processes, such as initiating, planning, executing, monitoring, and closing the project.

    Project Management Phases

  • Initiation: Define the project at a high level, including goals, feasibility, and scope. This phase often involves creating a project charter to document the project's purpose and requirements.
  • Planning: Develop a roadmap to achieve the project’s objectives, outlining timelines, budgets, and resources. This is where tasks are defined, schedules are set, and risk management plans are created.
  • Execution: Carry out the tasks defined in the plan, coordinating team members, managing resources, and ensuring deliverables are produced.
  • Monitoring and Controlling: Track the project's progress to make sure it aligns with the plan. This involves monitoring timelines, budgets, and quality to make adjustments as needed.
  • Closing: Conclude the project by delivering final products, releasing resources, and completing a project review. A final project report or evaluation helps assess success and lessons learned.

    Roles in Project Management

  • Project Manager (PM): Oversees the project’s scope, schedule, resources, and overall success.
  • Project Sponsor: Provides financial support and key decision-making for the project.
  • Team Members: Carry out project tasks under the project manager's guidance.
  • Stakeholders: Individuals or groups with a vested interest in the project’s outcome, such as clients or end-users.
